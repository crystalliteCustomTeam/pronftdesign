How do NFTs work?
NFTs are non-fungible tokens that exist on a blockchain. Unlike fungible tokens, which are interchangeable and have the same value, NFTs are unique, and each has its own weight.

NFTs can represent digital assets such as artwork, music, or even virtual real estate. Because they're stored on a blockchain, they can't be counterfeited or duplicated.

To create an NFT, you'll need to use a platform that supports them. Some popular platforms include Ethereum, Tron, and Wavelength. Once you've created your NFT, you can sell it or trade it on an online marketplace.
What Types Of Digital Assets Can Be Tokenized As NFTs?
One of the great things about NFTs is that they can be used to tokenize just about any type of digital asset, and this includes everything from art and music to in-game items and virtual real estate. In fact, there are already several platforms that allow you to buy, sell, or trade NFTs for a wide variety of different digital assets.

Some of the most popular types of NFTs include:

Artwork:

Perhaps the most obvious type of NFT, artwork can be tokenized and sold as an NFT. This includes everything from traditional paintings and sculptures to digital artworks created using software like Photoshop or Illustrator.

Music:

Music can also be tokenized as an NFT, which includes both digital recordings of songs and the musical compositions themselves. In some cases, music NFTs can even be used to represent a stake in future royalties earned by the song.

In-game items:

Many video games are now using NFTs to represent in-game items. This can include everything from cosmetic items like clothes and weapons to actual game pieces like land or buildings.

Virtual real estate:

NFTs can also represent ownership of the virtual real estate. This includes everything from virtual worlds like Second Life to online games like Decentraland.

These are just a few of the most popular types of NFTs. However, there are really no limits to what can be tokenized as an NFT. If it exists in digital form, chances are there's already an NFT for it – or one that will be created soon.
